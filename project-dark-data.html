OVERVIEW

Dark Data is a digital publication that focuses on the core ideas of elucidation, investigation and speculation of Dark Data. A web-zine written, edited and designed by students at Parsons School of Design for the course PSAM 5752 Dark Data taught by David Caroll & Melanie Crean. The publication touches on topics of privacy, surveillance, misuse of data and data speculations.

✸ HOW DID I GET THERE?
//VISUAL RESEARCH
MOOD BOARDS

We brainstormed some ideas for the project and we put together references of imagery, typography and other visual design elements as a mood board for the different visual directions we can take for the project.

VISUAL DIRECTIONS

We provided possible visual directions for the project to present to the class to vote on the desired direction. We wanted the process to be participatory and made sure to hear feedback from our peers. The visual ideas were categorized into 4 options.

▸ Option 1: Representation of the data matrix.
▸ Option 2: Hand-drawn data cluster.
▸ Option 3: Retro-brutalism style to represent the age before dark data.
▸ Option 4: News paper expose style to unravel the misuse of data.  ‍

//SITEMAP & WIREFRAMES
SITEMAP

Created the information architecture and wireframe for the website by collaborating with the editors. The developers started to build the CMS based on this while the class processed the designs, gave us feedback and each one voted for their visual style preference.  We decided to use mark-up format for article submissions to simplify the development process due to time considerations.

//DESIGN
HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

The newspaper expose style was the chosen visual direction. Using half-tone elements and color pallette from news-prints we designed the high-fidelity prototype. We designed desktop and mobile version to make the designs responsive.

//DESIGN SYSTEM & ARTWORKS
DESIGN SYSTEM

Based on the prototype, we developed the design system for the coders to base the development and to provide the mark-up formatting to the rest of the class for article submissions.

ARTWORKS

We worked individually with our peers to create artwork headers relevant to their articles for 12 articles in the same style using half-tones.
