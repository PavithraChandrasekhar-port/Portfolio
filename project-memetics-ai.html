OVERVIEW

Facebook conducted a challenge to build an open-source dataset for hateful memes. The dataset now constains 10000+ images. Social media platforms rely heavily on automated systems to moderate content. This project is an explorative study that analyzes 10000+ hateful memes submitted to this competition using artificial intelligence to identify and unearth similarities (if they exist), reflecting on the efficacy of the system in place to tackle hate speech.

DEFINING THE PROBLEM
PROBLEM STATEMENT

Social media platforms rely heavily on automated systems to curb hate speech. A few questions this project is trying to answer are as follows.

▸ Are machines equipped to classify hate speech memes?
▸ Can we unearth patterns in which hate is spread through memes?
▸ Will the analysis help identify hate speech in the future?

CONCEPT

Can we classify memes and identify visual similarities to aid machines to curb hate speech on conversational platforms?

//ML PROCESS
FEATURE EXTRACTION

Feature extraction is a machine learning methodology that breaksdown the data to extract information from the data.

K-MEANS CLUSTERING

K-means is a type of unsupervised learning that clusters unlabeled data together based on the similarity of its features.

//RESULTS & FUTURE STEPS
RESULTS

While you can see 2 clusters emerging on the right and left, there aren't any significant patterns in which hate speech is spread. Therefore making it harder to classify hate speech in memes as they are randomized and have infinite possibilities.

UNDERSTANDING HATE SPEECH THROUGH MEMES

The multi-modal method of spreading hate can use hate speech with random imagery or use contextual imagery with normal speech to that makes it hate speech. One of which is easier to classify than the other.
