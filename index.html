Overview

An interactive data-visualization tool revealing patterns of misinformation in India to increase data accesibility and understanding around misinformation. The on-going web-based project results from a data investigation on a self-generated database leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) techniques to analyze misinformation claims.

Leading Thesis Question

Misinformation Research has been overwhelmingly focused on the Global North. How can design interventions help curb misinformation in India?

④ Focused Generative
  • 07 Data Analytics
  • 08 Data Visualization
Scoping Framework

//01 Research

Began the research to understand the complexity of misinformation as a problem that's affecting the online sphere and having widespread social consequences by using a primarily exploratory methodology to identify pain points that can be addressed.

//02 Territory Mapping

Using territory mapping and card sorting the research was classified into four categories, namely:

  • • Understanding misinformation.
  • • Ethnographic Research to understand cultural factors, perceptions and behaviors.
  • • Technological Issues, reasons and motivations.
  • • Precedants and efforts to combat misinformation.

Early Exploratory

//03 Primary Research

Conducted an online survey with 356* respondants across age groups [18-55 & above] and political leanings from all over India.

The goal was to understand people's perception about, reactions to misinformation and the measures they take to protect themselves against it.


*The sample audience interviewed excludes many key groups.

//04 Stakeholder Interviews

After the online survey speaking to 5 people* over zoom about their experience with misinformation and how they are tackling it helped qualitatively understand how people understood and felt about the issue.


*The sample audience interviewed excludes many key groups.
Synthesis
① Acceptance to susceptibility
  • Indians shared with high frequency that they were exposed to misinformation and fake news. Even then, they found it difficult to recall their point of exposure.
② Display of hopelessness/fear
  • Even with awareness people find it difficult to meticulously identify if the information that they consume is true or false.
③ Breakdown of Social Platforms usage
  • Majority of the respondants used Whatsapp as their primary source of online information consumption, followed by Youtube and then Facebook
④ Fact-checking and Verified Sources
  • People were unaware of their own biases while gauging fake news, however they rely on fact checkers and verified sources.
Early Generative

//05 Data Accessibility

The primary and secondary research helped identifying the need to increase accesibility to data surrounding misinformation. Can using IFCN Certified fact checking websites that researchers, journalists, technologists, and citizens rely on to build a tool that reveals the patterns within debunked information by leveraging artificial intelligence techniques help the audience understand the focal points of widespread misinformation within the country?

//06 Data Scraping

Using Python and Beautiful Soup I wrote scripts to webscrape, the links, claims, date, year and the source of debunked information from independant websites and combined them manually into a single file.

The process of data acquisition is completely transparent and you can find the code → here.

Focused Generative

//07 Data Analytics

The goal for this analysis was to find patterns within this corpus drawn from IFCN certified fact-checking websites. A set of algorithms that I wrote performed tokenization, lemmitization and stop words removal on the data, to model the dataset for analysis.


You can find the code → here.

//08 Data Visualization

To present the findings from the analysis I visualized the data to effectively communicate it to the larger audience using HTML, CSS and Javascript.


You can find the code → here. and the project → here.

Overview

Darka Data is a digital publication that focuses on the core ideas of elucidation, investigation, and speculation of Dark Data. This project was a zine issue produced for the class "Dark Data" at Parsons School of Design taught by David Carroll and Melanie Crean in Spring 2021 where I led the interface design for the website, alongside Nishra Ranpura.

Discover

//01 Understanding Requirements

Design a web-zine for a publication written and edited by students at Parsons School of Design on critical investigations of dark data. The publication touches on topics of privacy, surveillance, misuse of data and data speculations.

Ideate

//02 Mood Boarding

To brainstorm some ideas post understanding the requirement for the project we put together references of imagery, typography and other visual design elements as a moodboard for the different visual directions.

//03 Visual Design Options

We provided initial options based on the moodboards and ideation to receive feedback from peers and faculty before moving ahead with the designs.

Define

//04 Sitemap & Wireframing

Meanwhile, we developed the information architecture and wireframing to make the website functional to the visual design elements we were creating.

//05 High-fidelity Prototype

The newsprint idea was selected to be further developed as it best represented the exposè factor in the tone of many of the articles published. We decided to leverage the dither and halftone elements of newsprint for the final outcome.

We created high-fidelity prototypes for the website (mobile &desktop) using figma.

Development

//06 Design System

We worked closely with developers Olivier Bruckner and Ege Uz. For design implementations, we created a design system that worked across the website. We also created thumbnail artworks for each article to maintain consistency across design

//07 Quality Testing

We tested the responsiveness and functionality across devices once the development was finished to ensure the quality of the final outcome.

Overview

An integrated android solution to affect positive behavioral changes for mobile users to help promote mindfulness, curb excessive usage and create a healthy balance and focus. This project was created at Parsons School of Design in collaboration with One Plus. The brief was to understand and design for digital efficiency and mindfulness of Gen-Z users.

  • 03 Proto-persona
  • 04 Competitive Analysis
② Define
  • 05 User Interviews
  • 06 User Journey Map
  • 07 Feature Analysis

③ Ideate
  • 08 Idea Vignettes
  • 09 Wireframing


④ Development
  • 10 Hi-fi prototypes



Discover

//01 Problem/Brief

Many Gen-Z users need help with focus and struggle with time-management. However, there are very few tools to help them find the balance between their offline life (work, family & friends) and digital (browsing, social media, miscellaneous.

//02 Initial Understanding

① Unique Usage Patterns
  • Each user's usage is unique from one another and desire different things based on their goals but striking a balance is a focal point for all of them.


② Dark patterns in Design
  • Applications manipulate users. They are designed using studies of psychological factors that use similar mechanisms of gambling that affect users to addict them to certain applications and games.


③ Mobile Phones as an Obstruction
  • Phone's for Gen-Z have migrated from tool of connectivity to a tool of distraction and other feelings of unpleasantness. More people are inclined to taking media breaks & digital detoxes today. However, our lives are hyper-linked to it and they are an unavoidable device.
④ Difficulty Unwinding
  • Blue light has effects on your sleep and more. It has properties that make you less drowsy at night and makes you longer to fall asleep.



//03 Proto-Persona

Based on the initial understanding of the users to build proto-personas that represent the audience to design for.

//04 Competitive Analysis

Defining the scope and nature of the industry, identifying the key players and evaluating their strengths and weakness.

Define

//05 User Interviews

Objective of the interviews was to determine usage patterns across age groups within Gen-Z users and identify pain ponts to better their experience.

Interviewees: Aditya Chandrasekhar, Sanjay Venkataramani, Samyuktha Karthik, Swetha Mohan, Harini Raghavan

//06 User Journey Map

Using the responses from the interview I mapped the sample journey of a user from day to night.

//07 Feature Analysis

Users found it ironic to download more apps to use to control mobile usage. The interviews revealed that they preffered in-built solutions. So, I further conducted user tests to understand how users felt about digital wellbeing addition on android.

Ideate

//08 Idea Vignettes

Using the design sprint methodology 'Crazy-8' I plotted ideas on to an impact/value chart to decide which ideas to move forward with.

//09 Wire-Framing

Created the schematic for the flow of the design for the selected designs.

Development

//10 High Fidelity Prototypes

The high fidelity prototypes were created using Google Material Design to keep the design language in congizance to the original.
