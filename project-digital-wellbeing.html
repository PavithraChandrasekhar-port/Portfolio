OVERVIEW

An integrated android application to affect positive behavioral changes for mobile users to help promote mindfulness, curb excessive usage and create a healthy balance. This project was created at Parsons School of Design in collaboration with One Plus as part of the AOD (Always On Display) Challenge.

✸ HOW DID I GET THERE?
//PLANNING & INSIGHTS
OBJECTIVE SETTING

Many Gen-Z users are increasingly needing help with focus. They struggle with time-management and screen-time addiction. However, there are very few tools to help them find the balance between their offline life (school, work, family & friends) and digital life (browsing, social media, miscellaneous).

▸ How can we design for the digital efficiency and mindfulness of Gen-Z users?

INITIAL UNDERSTANDING

First, I used secondary research methods to indentify and understand the problem.

//RESEARCH
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Conducting a market analysis for products available today that address mental health and digital wellbeing. By identifying direct & indirect competitors and comparing the product offerings, target audience, and doing a SWOT analysis for each product helping understand the marketspace for the problem.

PROTO-PERSONA

Creating variants of the typical target audience for the study/product based on assumptions made on who will interact with products for digital wellbeing.

USER INTERVIEW

To verify and collect more data on the target audience, I used the proto-personas to identify stakeholders to conduct interviews. The objective of the interview was to determine usage patterns in users and identify pain ponts to better their experience. The participants were selected across age groups among GenZ (born before 2000 & born after 20000)

USER JOURNEY MAP

Stakeholders phone usage in a day.

//SYNTHESIS
KEY PROBLEMS

Identifiying pain points and possible opportunities from the interviews and user journey map. The user research revealed three main problem areas that need to be adressed among Gen-Z users.

FEATURE ANALYSIS

Reviewing competitors for existing features that address these problem areas to identify opportunity areas for product development.

//CONCEPT & DEVELOPMENT
OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFYING

After synthesizing the research, I found that there were 3 key problem areas and how the current market space addressed these issues. I noticed a gap in the way that mindfulness and phone usage are percieved as two seperate problems and solutions are designed accordingly. To trigger different ideas and concepts I posed these opportunity areas as questions.

BRAINSTORMING

Brainstorm ideas and concepts and user feasibility mapping.
