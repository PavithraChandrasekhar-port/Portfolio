OVERVIEW

//RESEARCH
SYSTEMS MAPPING

The impact of misinformation on the information ecosystem is severe and challenging. Conspiracy theories, fake news, disinformation campaigns, manipulated media, and deep fakes have continued to challenge information ecosystems. Social media technologies use algorithms that have prioritized engagement over accuracy of information. Critical disinformation studies has aided in contextualizing, measuring and mitigating misinformation. Currently, research in this area has been primarily focused on the global north. The issue in India has led to mob killings, suppression of movements and more.voices.

The systems map of the information ecosystem tries to demystify the complexity of misinformation. It tries to break down misinformation to identify where design interventions can help curb the spread in India. The goal was to understand how different players in the misinformation landscape and their role in the misinformation sphere.



▸ What are the different types of misinformation?
▸ What are the different ethonographic factors (India) and how do they cause the spread of misinformation?
▸ What are the efforts being taken specifically to address different types of misinformation in this particular ethnography?
▸ What are the technological factors that play a role in the spread of misinformation and how does it tie into the misinformation ecosystem in India?
USER UNDERSTANDING

Misinformation is shared intentionally and unintentionally by people among their networks. A survey was designed to understand people's attitudes, reactions and opinions on misinformation. The survey was followed by an interview to provide a holistic understanding of the current landscape amongst the public that is both quantitative and qualitative.

The participants of the research where selected across age groups, gender, political leanings, and geographies within India.

▸ What are the information consumption habits of people in India? 
▸ What is their awareness towards misinformation? 
▸ What are the individual practices users take to shield themselves from misinformation? ‍


Limitations: This research was conducted to analyze perceptions and is not indicative of how the entire Indian population perceives misinformation but a representation that increases our understanding of the landscape.

//IDENTIFYING PAIN POINTS & TARGET USERS
MAIN CHALLENGES

The research revealed high rates of acceptance (77%) towards encountering misinformation. The largest market for Facebook and WhatsApp is India. Whatsapp (53.1%), which is one of facebook's entities was observed to be widely used for new consumption, followed by Youtube (35.7%).

Respondents shared with high frequency that they were exposed to misinformation and fake news. Even then, they found it difficult to recall their point of exposure. Even with awareness people find it difficult to meticulously identify if the information that they consume is true or false. People heavily rely on fact checkers to verify their information.

Misinformation is unstructured and communities face difficulty identifying their point of exposure. Individual biases make people susceptible to believing and amplifying misleading content. Solving problems starts with gaining comprehension of the available information but social media platforms rarely provide data while playing a massive role in amplifying misinformation which limits our understanding of the topic. 

KEY INSIGHTS

TL;DR

Misinformation is a complex problem area, there are two main problems that I'm trying to address through this project. Gaining access to misinformation data and increasing the comprehension around misinformation. The identified audience that'd benefit from this are researchers, designers, technologists, cautious information consumers, and users who want to be more aware.

//DESIGN CONCEPT
CONCEPT

A regularly updated data-visualization platform/tool that aims to contextualize misinformation by providing data access and insights to researchers and users, and tracks the changing trends of the misinformation content in India.

CREATING DATA ACCESS

Fact checking websites host accumulated information on debunked information. I used these seemingly disparate pieces of information to extract data from prominent IFCN certified fact checking websites in India.

//PROCESS
DATA SCRAPING

Performing web-scrapes on IFCN certified fact checking websites using Python & Beautiful Soup to create a dataset containing the debunked claims, date, year and links to the claims.

DATA ANALYTICS

A set of algorithms using Python and NLTK (Natural Language Processing) were written to perform text analysis within the corpus created from fact checking websites and find patterns in them.

DATA VISUALIZATION

The patterns found within the corpus were integrated into the dataset to create a platform that reveals these patterns in a digestible manner to a larger audience. The data was visualized using D3js.
